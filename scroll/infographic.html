Recycling Correctly




The act of putting something that seems recyclable in the bin in good faith is a common practice.

Unfortunately, often this ends up doing more harm as those objects tend to contaminate the whole bin, causing it to be landfilled.

Furthermore, the triangular "Recycling Logo" does not mean an object is recyclable, it is for identifying the type of plastic used. Only Number 2, and Number 5 Plastics can be commerically recycled.

Reducing Consumption






Our society is fueled by consumption and products are purposely designed to be disposed of.

What was once a breakthrough in convinience, is now a pressing issue that can no longer be ignored.

Recycling should be a last resort for waste, not a method for justifying increased consumption.

Consumer
Choices

Loop


Loop is a company that's parterned with major brands in the US, and ships products to shoppers in reusable metal jars. Buyers pay a deposit on the containers which is refunded once the containers are "looped" back to the company for future orders. Their products range from desserts to snacks to household items. Visit their site here

Precious Plastics




A startup intent on changing the way we see plastic's value. They've designed an open source machine which extrudes and molds plastic into proucts of any shape and color.
HDPE and PET can be fed into a shredder which melts it down and presses it into shape. Some artists have built their business entirely around products created with this method of reusing plastic.

Terracycle


A parent company of Loop, Terracycle was created as a service for recycling hard-to-recycle products into new products. They accept items ranging from used car seats, tide detergant bottles, water filters and more.

Carbios Recycling



Carbios is a french Biorecycling company which has recently tested that their enzyme is effective in breaking down plastics under heat. They are still refining the process and working to make it scalable, but the results thus far have been very promising.

EPR


EPR is the concept that requires manufacturers to be responsible for the treatment or proper disposal of products in the post consumer phase.

As a result, companies would be pressured to create efficient, possibly reusable packaging, rather than hard-to-recycle plastic containers.

Voting


Voting gives citizens tremendous power to create change. Elected officials play a huge part in passing legislation involving plastic at the local, state, and national level.
Electing the right officials, those who will listen to the demands of their constituents, will be critical in solving the plastic crisis.

Consolidated Recycling


A flaw with the recycling system in the US is that each municipality and town, in some cases, have their own recycling rules. Naturally this could lead to confusion if people are away from home, and ultimately the contamination of recycling loads.
An important first step to overhauling our recycling infrastructure would be to agree upon the same set of rules and standards nationwide.
